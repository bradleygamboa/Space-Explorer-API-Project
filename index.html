Mars Exploration Rovers

Spirit & Opportunity

NASA's Mars Exploration Rover Mission (MER) is an ongoing robotic space mission involving two rovers, Spirit and Opportunity. It began in 2003 with the sending of the two rovers, Spirit and Opportunity, to explore the Martian surface and geology. The mission's scientific objective was to search for and characterize Martian geology and soil chemistry that holds clues to past water activity on Mars. Each rover had a planned 90 day sol with each rover far exceeding it's end mission date and continuing to relay data to Earth. Unfortunately, Spirit became stuck in late 2009, and its last communication with Earth was sent on March 22, 2010. On the opposite side of Mars, Opportunity continues to move, gather scientific observations, and report back to Earth, exceeding its designed lifespan almost 50-fold.

Curiosity

Curiosity is a car sized robotic rover that landed on Mars August 6, 2012. It is the largest and most advanced rover sent to Space to date. After traveling over 350,000,000 miles from Earth, Curiosity landed a mere 1.5 miles from it's intended touchdown target. The Curiosity's mission is to investigate the Martian climate and geology, assess if Mars environmental conditions have ever been favorable for microbial life and acquire information regarding the planetary habitability in preparation for future human exploration. As of December 2012, Curiosity's 2-year mission was extended indefinitely and it continues to send home information about the barren Martian landscape to this day.
